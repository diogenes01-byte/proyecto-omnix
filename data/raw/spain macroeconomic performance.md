# Spain: Macroeconomic Performance and Structural Outlook

## Abstract

This report evaluates Spain’s macroeconomic performance between 2022 and 2025, with a focus on growth dynamics, labor market developments, fiscal sustainability, and structural challenges. It also assesses medium-term risks and policy priorities in the context of a tightening monetary environment and evolving global conditions.

---

## 1. Introduction

The Spanish economy demonstrated strong resilience following the pandemic recovery phase, supported by robust domestic demand, tourism activity, and European recovery funds. Between 2022 and 2023, Spain outperformed several euro area peers in terms of GDP growth.

However, by 2024–2025, economic momentum moderated due to tighter financial conditions, slowing external demand, and the fading impact of post-pandemic stimulus measures.

- Supported by employment growth and nominal wage increases

## 3. Inflation Dynamics

### 3.1 Headline and Core Inflation

- Peak inflation: ~10.8% in 2022  
- 2024 average: ~3.5%  
- 2025 estimate: ~2.6%  

Core inflation remained persistent, averaging around 3.2% in 2025.

---

### 3.2 Key Inflation Drivers

1. **Energy Prices**  
   Spain experienced a relatively faster decline in energy inflation compared to other euro area economies due to regulatory measures.

2. **Food Prices**  
   Continued to exert pressure, particularly in 2023.

3. **Services Inflation**  
   Driven by wage increases and strong demand in tourism-related sectors.

4. **Policy Measures**  
   Temporary VAT reductions and energy subsidies helped mitigate inflation but had fiscal implications.

## 4. Labor Market Developments

### 4.1 Employment Trends

- Unemployment rate declined from ~13.0% (2021) to ~11.5% in 2025  
- Job creation concentrated in services, particularly tourism and hospitality  
- Increase in permanent contracts following labor market reforms  

---

### 4.2 Structural Changes

The 2021 labor reform contributed to:

- Reduction in temporary employment  
- Increased job stability  
- Improved labor market resilience  

However, structural unemployment remains elevated relative to euro area peers.

---

### 4.3 Wage Dynamics

- Nominal wages increased by ~3.5–4.0% annually  
- Real wages declined initially due to inflation surge  
- Gradual recovery observed in 2024–2025  

Wage pressures contributed moderately to core inflation but did not trigger a wage-price spiral.

## 9. Conclusion

Spain’s economy has demonstrated resilience in the face of multiple shocks, supported by strong domestic demand and policy interventions. However, medium-term growth prospects depend on addressing structural weaknesses and maintaining fiscal discipline.

The transition toward a more sustainable and productive growth model remains a central policy objective.
