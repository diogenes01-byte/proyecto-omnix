## 2. Fixed Income Markets

### 2.1 Government Bond Yields

Sovereign bond yields increased significantly across major economies:

- US 10-year Treasury yields peaked around 4.5–5.0% in 2023–2024  
- German Bund yields rose from negative territory in 2021 to approximately 2.5–3.0%  
- Yield curves inverted in several economies, signaling recession risks  

#### Key drivers:

- Expectations of prolonged high policy rates  
- Inflation risk premia  
- Reduced central bank asset purchases (quantitative tightening)

---

### 2.2 Yield Curve Dynamics

Yield curve inversion became a prominent feature:

- Short-term rates exceeded long-term yields in major economies  
- Historically associated with future economic slowdowns  
- Reflects market expectations of eventual monetary easing  

The persistence of inversion into 2025 suggests continued uncertainty about growth prospects.

---

### 2.3 Corporate Bond Markets

Corporate credit conditions tightened:

- Investment-grade spreads widened moderately  
- High-yield spreads increased more significantly, reflecting higher risk aversion  
- Issuance volumes declined during peak tightening periods  

#### Implications:

- Increased financing costs for firms  
- Reduced access to capital markets for lower-rated issuers  
- Greater differentiation between credit quality segments

## 3. Equity Markets

### 3.1 Market Performance

Equity markets experienced volatility but showed resilience:

- Sharp correction in 2022 due to rising rates  
- Partial recovery in 2023–2024  
- Sectoral divergence remained pronounced  

Technology and growth stocks were particularly sensitive to interest rate changes.

---

### 3.2 Valuation Adjustments

Higher discount rates led to valuation compression:

- Price-to-earnings (P/E) ratios declined from elevated levels  
- Equity risk premium increased  
- Future earnings expectations adjusted downward  

However, strong corporate earnings in some sectors helped stabilize markets.

---

### 3.3 Sectoral Trends

#### Outperforming Sectors:
- Energy (benefiting from price shocks)  
- Financials (higher interest margins)  

#### Underperforming Sectors:
- Real estate (sensitive to interest rates)  
- Consumer discretionary (affected by reduced purchasing power)
